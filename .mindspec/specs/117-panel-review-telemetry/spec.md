# Spec 117-panel-review-telemetry: Durable, queryable capture of panel review verdicts + finding dispositions

## Goal

Make the output of every review panel â€” what each reviewer found, and crucially **how each finding was resolved** â€” a durable, queryable dataset instead of an evaporating byproduct. MindSpec runs multi-model panels at every gate (spec/plan â‰ˆ 9â€“12 slots, bead = 8, final = 12; all driven to unanimous approve under the findings-never-out-voted doctrine, `/ms-panel-tally` Â§ Anti-patterns). Each panel produces per-reviewer verdict JSONs, and the tally authority resolves every finding â€” fixed, deferred to a follow-up bead, refuted by isolated reproduction, or exposed as contamination. Today none of that resolution is captured in any structured form, so the questions that would let the panel design pay for itself with data â€” *what is each model's genuine-find rate? its false-positive rate? which gates yield findings per reviewer slot? how often do two lenses independently converge on the same real issue?* â€” cannot be answered. Raw finding counts are actively misleading without the disposition layer: they count noise, duplicates, and contamination as if they were genuine (the seed archive's README says exactly this, `/Users/Max/replit/mindspec-panel-verdicts/README.md:31-36`).

Target user outcome: **after every panel reaches its terminal state, one durable row per distinct finding â€” carrying the two orchestrator-decided fields the raw verdicts lack, `disposition` and `convergent_with` â€” exists in a store that is queryable across specs and sessions, written by the single tally authority as part of the gate flow it already runs, with no new manual step.** The spec-116 seed dataset (66 raw verdicts across 8 panels + 21 hand-authored disposition rows) migrates in as the first data points and as the schema-validation fixture. Claims like the seed's headline â€” "no single model dominated; diverse lenses caught different genuine issues" (README:38-47) â€” become checkable queries instead of anecdotes.

## Background

**What a panel writes today, and what survives.** `/ms-panel-run` step 0 registers the panel â€” `mindspec panel create` writes `<spec-dir>/reviews/<panel-slug>/panel.json` + `BRIEF.md` (`.claude/skills/ms-panel-run/SKILL.md:53-65`) â€” and each reviewer writes `<slot>-round-<N>.json` into that directory. The verdict-JSON contract is `verdict`, `hard_block`, `reviewer_id`, `confidence`, `rationale`, `concrete_changes_required`, `findings` (BRIEF machine-header, `internal/panel/create.go:203-205`); the binary parses only `verdict` + `hard_block` for the gate (`verdictJSON`, `internal/panel/tally.go:66-69`), everything else is presentation-only; the contract's canonical doc home is `.mindspec/domains/workflow/interfaces.md` Â§ Panel Artifact Schema (spec 110 Bead 1). Durability of those files is **an orchestrator habit, not a guarantee**: specs 110â€“115's panel dirs are git-committed under `.mindspec/specs/<spec>/reviews/` (~1000 tracked files, e.g. `.mindspec/specs/115-impl-approve-panel-gate/reviews/spec-115-final/`), and ad-hoc panels are tracked under `.mindspec/reviews/` (commit 6ca7bb08 "track ad-hoc review verdicts") â€” but spec 116's eight panels left **nothing** in the repo (zero git history for `.mindspec/specs/116-panel-message-escaping/reviews`): its panel/scratch artifacts lived under `/tmp` and in torn-down worktrees, and were rescued only by a hand-built sibling archive, `/Users/Max/replit/mindspec-panel-verdicts/`. That loss mode has since become the norm, not the exception: specs 118, 119, and 120 (all merged to main by 2026-07-19) ALSO have **no** `reviews/` dir in the repo â€” their raw verdicts survive only as `spec-118/`, `spec-119/`, `spec-120/` (and an in-flight `spec-121/`) subdirectories of the same sibling archive, none of which carries disposition rows. The archive is already functioning as the de-facto raw-verdict cold store, and the disposition gap keeps growing with every shipped spec. The adwu bead additionally records bead subagents' `git add -A` twice *deleting* an inherited review tree mid-spec (`bd show mindspec-adwu`). So even the raw layer is sometimes lost; and raw files across specs share no queryable schema.

**What is captured NOWHERE: the disposition.** The verdict JSONs record what a reviewer *claimed*. Whether the claim was **genuine** is decided later, by the panel's single decision authority â€” `/ms-panel-tally` (skill; `plugins/mindspec/skills/ms-panel-tally/SKILL.md`, materialized identically at `.claude/skills/ms-panel-tally/SKILL.md`) â€” as it consolidates `concrete_changes_required` (step 2, semantic dedupe + rank), drives fix rounds, applies audited refutations (Â§ halt-recovery item 5), and files follow-up beads. That judgment is exactly the data that makes a finding count meaningful, and today it evaporates: the closest artifacts are prose (`consolidated-round-<N>.md`, `DECISION-OUTCOME.md`) that no query can consume. The one existing structured capture is the hand-authored seed, `spec-116/DISPOSITIONS.jsonl` (21 rows), reconstructed after the fact â€” its README calls the disposition layer "the value-add" and warns that raw counts without it are misleading (README:13-16, 31-36).

**The seed dataset, audited (the schema-validation fixture).** 66 verdict JSONs across 8 panels (spec 9 slots, plan 9, four bead panels of 8, final 12, gapfix 4) + 21 disposition rows with fields `{spec, gate, panel, reviewer, model, severity, summary, convergent_with[], disposition, note}`. Verified distributions: dispositions = 13 confirmed-fixed, 1 confirmed-deferred, 1 confirmed-scope-trim, 3 deferred, 3 false-contamination, 0 audited-refuted; models = 5 fable, 6 opus, 7 sonnet, 3 gpt-5.6-sol; gates = 5 spec, 3 plan, 6 bead, 5 final, 2 gapfix; 4 rows carry non-empty `convergent_with`. Three schema deltas between the seed and the proposed capture schema, resolved by R2: (a) the seed has `note` (free prose, often embedding an evidence pointer â€” "Fixed in commit 5a0cfb24", "filed as mindspec-8f5l") where the proposal wants a structured `evidence_ref` â€” R2 keeps BOTH (`note` verbatim-migrated, `evidence_ref` optional and not force-extracted from prose); (b) the seed has no `created_at` â€” migration backfills it honestly (R5); (c) the seed's `gate` values (`spec`/`plan`/`final`/`gapfix`) are short forms of the canonical per-gate keys the binary already defines â€” `config.PanelGateKeys = ["spec_approve", "plan_approve", "bead", "final_review", "adhoc"]` (`internal/config/config.go:114`), the same enum `panel.json`'s decision-inert `gate` field records (`internal/panel/panel.go:77-91`, spec 112/113) â€” migration maps to canonical keys (R5).

**Row granularity â€” what "one row per finding" means.** The seed's 21 rows against 66 verdicts show the intended granularity: one row per **distinct** finding after the tally authority's semantic dedupe (skill step 2b), attributed to the primary reviewer, with the other independent raisers listed in `convergent_with` (which may name reviewers who have no row of their own â€” e.g. `S2` appears convergent on two bead-3a rows without its own row). APPROVE verdicts with no notable findings produce no rows. This judgment layer cannot be mechanically enforced row-for-row, but a mechanical **floor** can: every REQUEST_CHANGES/REJECT verdict of every round must be covered by at least one row naming its slot as `reviewer` or in `convergent_with` (R1). **Slot identity is the verdict-file token.** Verdict JSONs carry no populated `reviewer_id` (the field exists in the contract but reviewers leave it empty â€” the seed files confirm this), so the *filename slot token* (`verdict-<slot>.json` â†’ `<slot>`, e.g. `verdict-G1-codex.json` â†’ `G1-codex`) is the sole slot identity a check can key on. R1 therefore pins that a disposition row's `reviewer` and every `convergent_with[]` entry MUST byte-match a verdict-file slot token in their own panel. Because the raw verdict files themselves are NON-durable (OQ4/OQ5 leave them uncommitted / machine-local), the floor cannot depend on them at query time: R6 makes the tally authority persist a durable **per-panel coverage manifest** (every slot's token + model + verdict + the slot count) INTO the store, so both the floor and Q4's per-slot denominator are derivable from the durable store alone.

**Why capture belongs at the tally authority.** `/ms-panel-tally` is the ONE place every finding's fate is decided â€” the decision matrix, artifact-gate screen, consolidation, refutation and abandonment procedures all route through it (SKILL.md:8, "single decision authority"; both per-bead cycles and `/ms-spec-final-review` route their tally through it). The binary's `mindspec panel tally` verb mechanizes only the vote decision (`panelTallyCmd`, `cmd/mindspec/panel.go:232`); disposition is orchestrator judgment layered on top, per ADR-0040's layering (judgment in skills, mechanism in the binary). So the capture is a mandated step of the SKILL's flow â€” the same authority, at the same moments it already resolves findings â€” with the binary/script limited to validation/query/append mechanism over the per-panel JSONL store under each spec's reviews dir (OQ1 resolved; the only sub-choice left is verb-vs-script, bound at plan time). "At the moment it resolves the finding" is honest about time: dispositions land across the tallyâ†’fixâ†’re-panel loop (a `confirmed-fixed` is known at re-panel; a `deferred` may be known only when the follow-up bead is filed at close), so R1's completeness obligation binds at the panel's **terminal state**, with incremental writes before that.

**Where the durable store lives â€” the fork, RESOLVED: JSONL, one file per panel (OQ1).** The owner (Max) resolved this fork on 2026-07-20: the store is **append-only JSONL, git-versioned under each spec's reviews dir**, riding the same lifecycle auto-commits that already persist spec artifacts. The panel spec-approve review then refined the *layout within that decision* (below): **one file per panel**, `.mindspec/specs/<spec>/reviews/<panel>/dispositions.jsonl`, each file carrying its disposition rows plus one `record: "panel"` coverage manifest line written at terminal state (records are distinguished by the `record` field, not by position; the disposition-row shape stays seed-compatible; the migration re-lays the seed's single `DISPOSITIONS.jsonl` into per-panel files, R4). "Queryable" means glob (`.mindspec/specs/*/reviews/*/dispositions.jsonl`) + jq, plus a small machine-checkable Go/script surface for the Q1â€“Q5 computations, the validator, the completeness check, and the canonical append op â€” NOT SQL. Three candidate homes were weighed, each grounded: (a) **bd's existing Dolt DB** â€” embedded, in-process at `.beads/embeddeddolt` (`bd dolt status`; `.beads/metadata.json` `dolt_mode: embedded`), SQL-queryable, synced via `bd dolt push` to the same public GitHub remote (`.beads/config.yaml` `sync.remote`); **REJECTED** because the schema is owned by bd upstream (bd 1.1.0 â€” a foreign table must survive bd migrations), bd exposes no verb for custom tables (writes would be raw `dolt sql` against bd's live embedded store, contending with the shared server at `~/.beads/shared-server`), and â€” decisively â€” **worktrees do not share a live Dolt database with main** (`.beads/config.yaml`'s own export.git-add comment) while panels are tallied INSIDE spec worktrees, so the write path is structurally hostile (the spec-116 loss mode). (b) **Append-only JSONL under `.mindspec/`** â€” git-versioned, greppable/jq-queryable, rides the same lifecycle auto-commits that already persist spec artifacts â€” **SELECTED**. *Layout, one file per panel:* a single per-spec `DISPOSITIONS.jsonl` is merge-safe only CROSS-spec â€” parallel bead worktrees/panels WITHIN one spec would all append to the same file's EOF and git-conflict (or force a hand-merge that silently drops rows), so the store shards to `<spec-dir>/reviews/<panel>/dispositions.jsonl` (one writer per file, merge-safe within a spec; cross-panel/cross-spec reads glob). (c) **Both** â€” JSONL as the agent-writable write-time source of truth, ingested into a SQL surface for analysis; **REJECTED** because it buys SQL only at the cost of an ingest step that can drift, and the Q1â€“Q5 surface is cheaply served by jq + a small Go/script surface over the JSONL without it. **The durability hole the panel closed, and how.** Both load-bearing properties â€” the R1(b) completeness floor and Q4's per-slot denominator â€” need the set of RC/REJECT slots and the slot COUNT per panel, which live only in the raw verdict files that OQ4 declines to commit and OQ5 leaves machine-local; so the durable JSONL could survive while the evidence to prove its own floor / compute Q4 disappeared. The fix (R6): each per-panel `dispositions.jsonl` carries a **`record: "panel"` coverage manifest** `{record:"panel", spec, gate, panel, round, slots:[{slot, model, verdict}], backfilled}` capturing every slot's token + model + terminal verdict + (by count) the slot roster, path-scrubbed (R5); its other lines are `{record:"disposition", â€¦}` finding rows. The floor and Q4's denominator both derive from the manifest â€” no raw-verdict commit needed, consistent with OQ4. **Write lifecycle + mechanism (R6, gate-before-mutate).** ONE coherent append-only lifecycle: disposition rows are appended incrementally as findings resolve, and the manifest is appended once at terminal state (when terminal coverage is known) â€” records are keyed by the `record` field, so the manifest needs no fixed position. ONE canonical append operation (a `mindspec` verb under `cmd/` + `internal/panel/` OR a tracked script â€” the one remaining sub-choice, bound at plan time) performs every write: it validates schema + R5 hygiene BEFORE any file mutation (ADR-0041; exit-non-zero â‡’ nothing written), appends atomically under concurrent invocation, and is retry-idempotent â€” rows on their content-derived `id`, the manifest on `{spec, panel, round}`; the `/ms-panel-tally` skill invokes only that op. Everything lands publicly either way (the R5 public-repo posture is unchanged). One non-candidate, named to preempt confusion: `internal/recording` (core-owned) is the AgentMind OTLP session-recording bootstrap (ADR-0027's otel-only surface) â€” a live-session event stream, not a durable queryable dataset â€” and is not a home for this store.

**Trust model â€” same as every panel artifact.** Disposition rows are orchestrator-authored repo data, agent-writable and forgeable-by-content exactly like `panel.json` and the verdict files (ADR-0037 Â§8). This spec adds telemetry about the process, not authentication of it; rows are decision-inert with respect to the gate (the ADR-0037 decision matrix consumes none of this â€” same posture as `panel.json`'s decision-inert `gate` field, `panel.go:83-91`), unless and until OQ2 deliberately adds an advisory.

**Already-shipped baseline (specs 116/119/120 â€” consumed, not re-specified).** Three things this spec's tooling inherits rather than builds: (1) the terminal-escaping mechanism â€” `internal/termsafe.Escape` (spec 116) plus spec 120's repo-wide render-provenance discipline (idrender forced-safe sinks, the `FormatText` choke-point backstop, ADR-0042) â€” so any validator/query verb or script that renders JSONL row content (reviewer-authored `summary`/`note`/`reviewer_id` are agent-writable, exactly the untrusted-provenance class) MUST route through the existing safe-render surfaces, and spec 120's `internal/lint` go/ast ratchet scans (Bead 4 R6, workflow-owned) will mechanically flag raw-id/raw-string renders in any new Go code here; (2) the gate-before-mutate ordering contract (ADR-0041, spec 119) â€” R6's canonical append op (verb or script) must derive and deliver its refusals (schema-invalid row, unknown gate key, hygiene violation) BEFORE any file mutation; (3) the advisory-Warn-at-complete precedent â€” spec 119 shipped a termsafe-escaped, non-fatal bead-scope WARN inside `mindspec complete`, which is the shape OQ2 would reuse if it is ever taken up. None of 116/119/120 captures dispositions or any panel telemetry; every Requirement below remains unshipped.

## Requirements

1. **Capture at the single decision authority, as part of the flow it already runs â€” complete by terminal state, with a mechanical floor.** `/ms-panel-tally`'s mandated steps (in BOTH skill surfaces, which must stay byte-identical) gain the disposition write: as the tally authority resolves each finding â€” consolidation (step 2), fix-round adjudication, refutation (Â§ halt-recovery item 5), follow-up filing â€” it appends (via R6's canonical op) one durable row per distinct finding to that panel's file, `.mindspec/specs/<spec>/reviews/<panel>/dispositions.jsonl` (one file per panel; merge-safe within a spec). **Slot-identity contract:** the verdict-file token (`verdict-<slot>.json` â†’ `<slot>`) is the sole slot identity; a disposition row's `reviewer` and every `convergent_with[]` entry MUST byte-match a slot token present in that panel's coverage manifest (R6). No new operator-facing manual step exists outside the skill's own flow. Completeness binds at the panel's **terminal state** (gate Allow handed to the merge terminal, or audited abandonment): at that point, (a) every distinct finding the authority adjudicated has a row, and (b) â€” the mechanical floor â€” every slot whose terminal verdict in the R6 coverage manifest is REQUEST_CHANGES or REJECT is covered by at least one row naming that slot as `reviewer` or in `convergent_with[]`. A machine-checkable completeness check implementing floor (b) exists (a workflow-owned `mindspec` verb or tracked script) and computes the floor from the durable per-panel manifest ALONE â€” not from the raw verdict files, which are non-durable (OQ4/OQ5). Rows for advisory findings inside APPROVE verdicts are permitted and encouraged ("notable" findings, the seed's practice) but not floor-mandated. **Falsified if**: a panel driven through `/ms-panel-tally` to terminal state can leave a manifest RC/REJECT slot with no covering row and the completeness check still passes; if the completeness check, run against the migrated spec-116 store with one covering row deleted, does NOT fail; if the check consults raw verdict files rather than the durable manifest; or if the two skill surfaces diverge.
2. **The row schema â€” sufficient for every seed row, machine-validatable.** One disposition row per distinct finding, tagged `record: "disposition"`: `{record, id, spec, gate, panel, reviewer, model, severity, summary, convergent_with[], disposition, evidence_ref, note, created_at, round?, backfilled}` where: `record` is the discriminator, `record âˆˆ {"disposition", "panel"}` (a disposition row is `"disposition"`; the R6 coverage manifest is `"panel"`) â€” any other `record` value is REJECTED; `id` is a stable content-derived row identifier (e.g. a hash of `{spec, panel, reviewer, round, summary}`) that makes R6's append retry-idempotent; `gate` âˆˆ `config.PanelGateKeys` (`spec_approve | plan_approve | bead | final_review | adhoc`, `internal/config/config.go:114`); `reviewer` byte-matches a verdict-file slot token in the panel's R6 coverage manifest (R1 slot-identity contract); `model` is the ACTUAL model that produced the verdict (open string; canonical values today `fable | opus | sonnet | gpt-5.6-sol` â€” actual, not slot-family, so a claude-sub substitution in a codex slot (`ms-panel-run` SKILL.md:181) is attributed to the substituting model and codex-effectiveness queries stay honest); `disposition` âˆˆ `{confirmed-fixed, confirmed-deferred, confirmed-scope-trim, deferred, false-contamination, audited-refuted}` (closed enum, the seed README's definitions verbatim); `convergent_with[]` lists other reviewers who independently raised the same finding, each byte-matching a manifest slot token, and MAY name reviewers with no row of their own; `evidence_ref` (optional) points at the fix commit / follow-up bead / refutation record; `note` (optional) is free prose; `created_at` is required on newly captured rows (RFC 3339); `round` (optional) is the verdict round the finding first appeared in; `backfilled` is a required boolean that is `true` on migrated seed rows and `false` on live-captured rows (the named provenance marker â€” migration rows are structurally distinguishable, and the validator checks it). Derived metric definitions are pinned HERE so every consumer agrees: **genuine** := disposition âˆˆ {confirmed-fixed, confirmed-deferred, confirmed-scope-trim}; **false-positive** := disposition âˆˆ {false-contamination, audited-refuted}; plain `deferred` is accepted-but-unadjudicated and counts in NEITHER numerator (denominator only). A schema validator exists (a workflow-owned `mindspec` verb or tracked script over the JSONL) that accepts every migrated seed row and every migrated manifest, and enforces this **completeness property: no malformed disposition row OR coverage manifest passes** â€” including a bad `record` discriminator (âˆ‰ `{"disposition","panel"}`), a missing or wrong-type value in any required field, a wrong-type value in any PRESENT optional field (`evidence_ref`/`note`/`round`), and â€” for a manifest â€” a malformed nested `slots[]` entry (see R6(a): each entry's `slot`/`model`/`verdict` validated by type and, for `verdict`, by enum). The exhaustive per-field/per-nested-field negative-fixture enumeration binds at plan time (per the AC-discipline preamble); the completeness property is pinned HERE. **Falsified if**: any of the 21 seed rows cannot be expressed without information loss (`note` prose truncated, a gate value unmappable, a convergent_with entry dropped); if the validator accepts `disposition: "fixed"`, a gate key outside `PanelGateKeys`, a `record` outside `{"disposition","panel"}`, a non-RFC-3339 `created_at`, a non-array `convergent_with`, or a manifest with a malformed `slots[]` entry; or if any consumer computes "genuine" with a different disposition set than the one pinned here.
